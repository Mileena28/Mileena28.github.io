Community

The stories that find me aren't easy ones to hold. This page exists because some stories demand to be witnessed. Not consumed as entertainment, not collected as inspiration, but truly seen and honored for what they are: acts of profound courage from people who refuse to let their pain exist in isolation.

What started as a simple invitation—"your voice matters, your story matters"—has become something I never expected. A space where survivors of religious trauma, medical abuse, childhood neglect, domestic violence, and systemic betrayal find the courage to break decades of silence. Where shame transforms into strength, where isolation gives way to connection, where one person's truth creates permission for others to speak theirs.

These aren't just stories. They're lifelines thrown across the darkness between one survivor and another. They're proof that healing is possible, that survival has meaning, that the very experiences meant to destroy us can become the foundation for protecting others.

Every account shared here represents someone choosing vulnerability over safety, truth over silence, healing over hiding. They've trusted me with their most painful memories, and I've tried to honor that trust by holding their words with the reverence they deserve.

If you're here because you recognize yourself in these experiences, know this: you're not alone in carrying what you carry. Your story matters too. And when you're ready—if you're ever ready—there are people who will believe you, support you, and remind you that surviving is sacred work.

Fiction That Tells Our Truth

Blood of the Wild Gods: The Chosen Chronicles

Dark fantasy that refuses to rush healing, sanitize abuse, or pretend that trauma resolves in three acts. This is the 18-year journey we never see in fiction.

4 Books • 35+ Chapters • 128K+ Words
Currently writing Book 2: The Forsaken
Where religious control meets resistance, where trauma becomes power, where an 18-year journey proves that healing is the ultimate rebellion

For those who know what it's like when faith becomes a weapon, when love becomes control, when family becomes the first to turn away.

The Chosen Chronicles follows Mira Roehart through an 18-year journey that begins when a charismatic religious leader arrives in her town. What starts as a promise of belonging becomes systematic entrapment—forced marriage disguised as divine will, marital rape justified as "wifely duty," and the slow erosion of every relationship that once anchored her to herself. Her father chooses the faith over his daughter. Her best friend transforms into a zealous stranger. The town that raised her becomes a cage of surveillance and judgment.

This series doesn't rush to easy vengeance or quick healing. Book One shows the systematic destruction of identity through religious control. Book Two follows a pregnant survivor finding dangerous sanctuary among other outcasts. Book Three—set years later—isn't about fighting but about thriving, showing how healing happens in spirals, not straight lines, as Mira raises her son in magical sanctuary far from those who hurt her. Only in Book Four, after genuine healing and growth, does she choose to return—not from a place of rage, but from a place of power.

"Written by a former Jehovah's Witness and trauma advocate, this series includes authentic portrayals of dissociation during assault, the confusion of unwanted physical responses, religious trauma and spiritual abuse recovery, the isolation tactics of high-control groups, and why leaving is never as simple as just walking away. Every scene of trauma is written to validate, not sensationalize. Every step of healing honors how long real recovery takes. This is fantasy that tells the truth about survival."

Content includes: Religious extremism and cult dynamics • Marital rape and sexual coercion (Chapters 16-19 of Book 1 contain explicit scenes) • Realistic trauma responses including unwanted physical arousal during assault • Family abandonment and betrayal by loved ones • Systematic gaslighting, isolation, and identity destruction • Complex PTSD, dissociation, and suicidal ideation • Pregnancy from rape that becomes unexpected source of purpose • Public execution and mob violence • The long, non-linear journey of learning to trust again • Found family and the fierce loyalty of chosen bonds • Reclaiming power after it's been systematically destroyed

Why this series exists: Too many of us have been told our stories are too dark, too complex, too uncomfortable for mainstream fiction. The Chosen Chronicles refuses to sanitize the survivor experience. Here, the father who chooses doctrine over daughter isn't redeemed by the end. The best friend who betrays you stays lost to extremism. The eighteen years it takes to heal and return stronger isn't condensed into a training montage. This is the fantasy series that trauma survivors have been waiting for—one that honors the full weight of what we've survived and the long, spiral journey toward reclaiming ourselves.
