Mileena Rayne

Author Musician RN Mother

"I write from scars that have become stories, sing from silence that became songs, and create from the chaos that once tried to consume me."

The Story Behind the Stories

I create dark fantasy and haunting music from lived experience—real, raw, and unfiltered. Former Jehovah's Witness turned trauma advocate, I believe in the power of uncomfortable truths and the beauty found in broken places.

My Journey Current Focus My Writing My Music

Who I Am

I'm Mileena Rayne—a Minnesota native who writes from the intersection of trauma and transformation. Everything I create comes from a place that once hurt: fractured homes, religious control, and belief systems that tried to reshape me into someone I wasn't.

I didn't start creating until nearly thirty. Not because I lacked stories, but because I was surviving. Once I began, I couldn't stop. Every lyric, every chapter carries memory—trauma, truth, grief, clarity. I don't make it comfortable. I make it honest.

What Drives Me

People call me "intense"—and they're right. I feel deeply, think constantly, and refuse to pretend something's okay when it's not. This intensity once felt like a flaw. Now I see it as my superpower: it's what lets me write stories that stay with people, show up fully for my patients, and speak up when it matters.

My passion for trauma-informed care drives everything. Whether in the hospital, through my writing, or in the survivor stories I share, I'm committed to creating spaces where pain is witnessed, not sanitized.

Former Jehovah's Witness & Religious Trauma Survivor

Born into the organization, I spent my formative years in Kingdom Halls, knocking on doors, believing I had "the truth." At eighteen, I made the hardest decision of my life: I left, knowing it would cost me my community, my worldview, and relationships with family and friends still inside.

"Leaving didn't just change my beliefs—it taught me that truth is worth any cost, even when that cost is everything you thought you knew about yourself."

This experience—layered with childhood trauma, family dysfunction, the pain of intimate relationships that left their own scars, medical challenges, and the everyday battles of survival—shapes everything I create. My dark fantasy explores not just cult psychology but the accumulated weight of wounds that compound over time. My music voices the complex grief of multiple losses: religious community, family bonds, innocence, trust in those we loved most, and the selves we had to abandon to survive. Each story and song carries the full spectrum of trauma and resilience—because healing isn't about one pivotal moment but the slow, patient work of integrating all our broken pieces.

As an ex-JW author and trauma survivor, I write for anyone who carries multiple wounds, anyone whose story can't be simplified to a single event. My goal is to create art that acknowledges the messy reality: that trauma accumulates, that healing spirals rather than ascending, and that some days just getting through is enough.

Worlds That Shaped Me

Growing up, I found solace in stories and music. When the real world felt unbearable, I escaped into books and games where I could be someone else, somewhere else. These weren't just entertainment—they were survival, teaching me that other worlds existed and that I could build my own.

Books That Raised Me

Eragon Series

Christopher Paolini showed me how a young person could create a beloved fantasy world so vivid it breathed on its own. Dragons understood being different, and through his pages I learned to love characters so deeply they became part of me.

Inkheart Series

Stories should be powerful enough to pull you in, to make you forget where reality ends and fiction begins. This series taught me that words have the power to create worlds—and sometimes, to escape them.

Kira-Kira

The first book that made me cry, teaching me that sad endings could be beautiful and necessary. That you can still love a book that breaks you. That beauty exists even in broken places, shimmering like its title—glittering.

Chronicles of Narnia

C.S. Lewis taught me to imagine more worlds than I thought possible, how to fall in love with diverse characters, and most importantly, how to truly fall in love with the world you create. Sometimes you have to leave everything you know to find where you belong.

Games That Saved Me

EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers

My introduction to the wonderful world of online fantasy. The questlines told stories I engraved into my memory. My formative years were spent living in Tunaria, where the world I built online felt safer than the one outside my door.

Final Fantasy Online

Where I created my found surname: Rayne. For years I lived in that online community, and that last name became who I really was—more real than the identity I wore in daylight. It taught me about community in virtual spaces, how strangers could become family.

Elder Scrolls Online & Skyrim

Where I truly absorbed how stories could become an interconnected puzzle spanning years. I couldn't stop picking up quests, collecting them like little treasures as I went along—each one revealing another piece of a larger world. Being a bard wandering ancient forests in Skyrim still haunts my soul, while ESO taught me patience in storytelling and the beauty of narratives that unfold across time.

Ark: Survival Evolved

Where I learned to survive and build something beautiful in a world full of death and danger. Every structure I raised was proof that creation is possible even in chaos—that you can make homes in hostile places.

These stories and worlds weren't just escapism—they were education. They taught me that being different was power, that broken things could be beautiful, that you could build homes in impossible places. Every book I read and every world I inhabited online became part of my foundation, teaching me not just how to survive, but how to create.

My Journey

Childhood

Lost in Other Worlds

Found refuge in books and video games, discovering that stories could be sanctuaries. My dad introduced me to fantasy realms where being different wasn't a flaw—it was power.

Age 18

Breaking Free

Left the Jehovah's Witnesses, shattering my entire worldview. Had to rebuild everything from nothing—searching, questioning, aching for what was real.

Nearly 30

Finding My Voice

Started writing and making music after years of processing. Discovered that everything I create comes from the life I've lived—stitched with memory, survival, and scars that never quite healed.

Present

Building Something Real

Registered Nurse, mother of two incredible daughters, dark fantasy author, and musician. Using my voice to speak for those who can't and creating art that refuses to look away from hard truths.

Current Focus

Thriving in Healthcare

After 8 months as a resource RN on medical-surgical floors, I'm ready to find my home. Currently pursuing a transition to operating room nursing where I can develop deep expertise and truly master my craft.

Amplifying Survivor Voices

My greatest fulfillment comes from sharing community survivor stories. Each story I write for others is a candle lit in someone's darkness—proof that healing is possible and no one walks alone.

Building My Fantasy World

Every moment of free time is spent immersed in Blood of the Wild Gods, crafting The Chosen Chronicles four-book series. With the entire plotline mapped—beautiful, tragic, and deeply symbolic—I write chapters daily or build out the world to make it breathe. I can't wait to hold these physical books with their stunning covers.

Long-Term Vision

Building connections with SANE nursing leadership at my organization. While becoming a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner remains my north star, I'm approaching it with patience—laying groundwork for when the timing aligns.
